Why Every Indian SME Should Migrate from Tally to Zoho Books in 2025
Accounting & Migration
Rahul Kumar
April 5, 2025
8 min read
Accounting, Migration

For over three decades, Tally has been the default accounting software for Indian businesses. It's reliable, familiar, and deeply embedded in the workflow of millions of finance teams. But the business world in 2025 looks nothing like it did in 1990. The question isn't whether Tally served you well — it's whether it's still the right tool for where your business is going.

"The businesses that will win in the next decade are those that can make decisions in real-time — not those waiting for an end-of-month Tally report."

1. Real-Time Access, Anywhere

Tally is installed software. Your finance data lives on a specific machine in your office. Zoho Books is cloud-native — your CFO can pull up your P&L from Dubai while your accountant reconciles bank feeds from Bangalore. For businesses managing teams across multiple cities or countries, this alone is a game-changer.

2. GST Automation Built-In

With Zoho Books, GST is not an add-on — it's baked into every transaction. The system auto-populates GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B, matches purchase records with supplier invoices, and flags mismatches before they become filing errors.

  • Automatic GST calculation across all invoices
  • Direct e-invoicing integration with the GST portal
  • Real-time reconciliation of input tax credits
  • One-click GSTR filing submissions

3. Integration With Your Entire Business

Zoho Books connects natively with Zoho CRM, Zoho Inventory, Zoho Expense, and 50+ other apps in the Zoho ecosystem. When a sale closes in CRM, an invoice is auto-generated in Books. When stock is dispatched from Inventory, COGS is updated automatically.

4. Multi-Currency & International Compliance

Zoho Books handles multi-currency transactions automatically, applies real-time forex rates, and generates reports compliant with both Indian and international accounting standards.

5. The Migration Is Simpler Than You Think

At Zolytics, we've migrated dozens of companies from Tally to Zoho Books — ledgers, transactions, opening balances, and custom configurations — with zero data loss and minimal downtime. Typically 2–4 weeks from start to go-live.

5 Zoho CRM Automations That Will Double Your Sales Pipeline Efficiency
CRM & Sales
Priya Sharma
April 6, 2025
6 min read
Zoho CRM, Automation

Most businesses using Zoho CRM use less than 30% of its capabilities. They log deals, track contacts, and maybe send a few emails. But the teams consistently closing 40–60% more deals? They've set up the right automations. Here are the five that make the biggest difference.

1. Lead Scoring & Auto-Assignment

Zoho CRM's lead scoring rules let you automatically rank incoming leads based on source, company size, engagement level, and behavior. High-score leads get routed directly to your senior reps within seconds — no manual triaging, no lost opportunities over a weekend.

"A lead that is followed up within 5 minutes is 21x more likely to convert than one followed up after 30 minutes."

2. Automatic Follow-Up Sequences

Create workflow rules that trigger a sequence of personalized emails and tasks when a lead moves to a specific stage. If a prospect downloads your pricing brochure, they automatically receive a follow-up email in 24 hours and a task for your rep to call in 48 hours.

3. Deal Stagnation Alerts

Deals that sit untouched for more than a set number of days get flagged automatically. Your sales manager receives an alert, the deal owner gets a task reminder, and the lead is tagged for a review call. No deal falls through the cracks silently again.

4. Quote-to-Invoice in One Click

When a deal is marked "Closed Won" in Zoho CRM, a workflow can automatically generate a quote in Zoho Books, send it to the client for e-signature via Zoho Sign, and create the invoice upon approval — all without anyone touching a keyboard.

5. Customer Health Score Monitoring

Set up a composite score based on last purchase date, support ticket volume, email engagement, and contract renewal proximity. When a customer's health score drops below a threshold, your account manager gets an alert to reach out proactively — before they churn.

  • Triggers: field updates, date-based, activity-based
  • Actions: emails, tasks, record updates, webhooks, Cliq alerts
  • Advanced: Blueprint for stage-gated sales processes

How to Automate GST Filing Completely Using Zoho Books — A Step-by-Step Guide
GST & Compliance
Amit Mehta
April 7, 2025
10 min read
GST, Zoho Books, Compliance

For most Indian businesses, GST filing is a monthly anxiety — a frantic scramble of Excel sheets, GSTN portal errors, and last-minute calls to your CA. Zoho Books, configured correctly, can automate the entire GST workflow from invoice creation to return filing. Here's how to do it.

Step 1: Configure Your GSTIN and Tax Rates

In Zoho Books, navigate to Settings → GST Settings. Enter your GSTIN, filing frequency (monthly/quarterly), and business type. Zoho Books will auto-populate the applicable GST slabs (5%, 12%, 18%, 28%) and apply the correct SAC/HSN codes to your items and services.

Step 2: Enable E-Invoicing Integration

If your turnover exceeds ₹5 crore, e-invoicing is mandatory. Zoho Books integrates directly with the Invoice Registration Portal (IRP). Every invoice you raise is automatically validated, IRN-generated, and QR-coded.

  • Auto-generation of IRN (Invoice Reference Number)
  • Dynamic QR code embedded on every invoice
  • Instant validation and error reporting
  • Bulk e-invoicing for high-volume businesses
"After switching to Zoho Books with automated GST, our monthly compliance time dropped from 3 days to just 4 hours." — A Zolytics client, Manufacturing sector, Pune

Step 3: Automate GSTR-1 Preparation

Zoho Books automatically categorises every sales transaction into the correct GSTR-1 tables: B2B, B2C Large, B2C Small, exports, credit notes, and amendments. At the end of each month, click "Prepare GSTR-1" and the form is populated — reviewed, not built from scratch.

Step 4: Reconcile Input Tax Credits (ITC)

Zoho Books auto-fetches your suppliers' GSTR-2A/2B data and matches it against your purchase register. Unmatched records are flagged for follow-up with vendors before the filing deadline. You claim every rupee of ITC you're entitled to.

Step 5: File GSTR-3B with One Click

Once your GSTR-1 is filed and ITC is reconciled, Zoho Books auto-computes your GSTR-3B liability. Review the summary, verify the tax payable amount, and submit directly through the GST portal integration.
