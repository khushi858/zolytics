How to Automate GST Filing Completely Using Zoho Books
GST & Compliance
Amit Mehta
April 7, 2025
10 min read

For most Indian businesses, GST filing is a monthly anxiety. Zoho Books, configured correctly, can automate the entire GST workflow from invoice creation to return filing.

Step 1: Configure GSTIN and Tax Rates

Go to Settings → GST Settings. Enter your GSTIN, filing frequency, and business type. Zoho Books auto-populates the applicable GST slabs and HSN/SAC codes.

Step 2: Enable E-Invoicing Integration

Zoho Books integrates directly with the Invoice Registration Portal (IRP). Every invoice is automatically validated, IRN-generated, and QR-coded.

Step 3: Automate GSTR-1 Preparation

Zoho Books auto-categorises every sales transaction into the correct GSTR-1 tables. At month end, click "Prepare GSTR-1" and the form is ready to review.

Step 4: Reconcile Input Tax Credits

Auto-fetch suppliers' GSTR-2A/2B data and match it against your purchase register. Unmatched records are flagged for vendor follow-up.

"After switching to Zoho Books with automated GST, our monthly compliance time dropped from 3 days to just 4 hours." — Zolytics client, Pune

Early Access

Zoho Inventory

  • Set different conversions from the same base unit for an item

Batch Price Management — Zoho Inventory Q1 2026
Pricing Control
Jan–Mar 2026
Zoho Inventory

You can now set different prices for your batch-tracked items instead of using a single price for selling all batch items. This ensures each sale reflects your preferred price even when batches vary in MRP.

Price Selection Options

  • Item's default price
  • Highest price across batches
  • Latest price
  • Price from rate list

You can also set sales margins at the item or organization level for default price calculation. During transactions, the system applies the right batch price and splits line items when multiple batches have different prices and quantities.

How to Enable

Go to Settings → Module Settings → Items → Advanced Inventory Tracking → Allow different MRP/Selling price for each Batch Tracked Items.

Profit Margin Calculation — Zoho Inventory Q1 2026
Premium & Enterprise
Jan–Mar 2026
Zoho Inventory
Premium+

Profit margin is the percentage of money you keep as profit from a sale after subtracting the cost price and any discount. This feature allows you to view estimated profit margins directly within Sales Orders and Invoices — giving you instant visibility into profitability before confirming a transaction.

Cost Price Sources

  • Item details
  • Purchase bills
  • Inventory Valuation Methods (FIFO and WAC)
  • Custom rate depending on item type

Where It Appears

Sales Orders, Invoices, and Receivables reports including Sales Order Details and Invoice Details.

How to Enable

Go to Settings → Under General → check Profit Margin.

This feature is available only for Premium and Enterprise plans.

Count Stocks on Web — Zoho Inventory Q1 2026
Warehouse Operations
Jan–Mar 2026
Zoho Inventory

Counting stocks is no longer limited to the mobile app. You can now start, perform, and submit stock counts directly from the web application.

Two Web Views

  • Items View: See your items in a list and enter the counted quantity for each item. Use filters, search, and scan barcodes to locate items quickly.
  • Bins View: Navigate through your bin locations and count the stock stored in each bin.

A detailed view of how stock moves across warehouses, customers, and vendors within a selected date range. Every transaction that impacts stock is captured, making it easy to trace whether movement was inward or outward.

Calculates turnover ratio as COGS / Average Inventory Value, helping you understand how effectively you're converting inventory investment into sales.

Calculates turnover ratio as Total Quantity Sold / Average Inventory Quantity — a volume-based view of inventory efficiency.
